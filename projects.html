OceanSim: A GPU-Accelerated Underwater Robot Perception Simulation Framework

I worked on implementing a Side Scan Sonar (SSS) sensor into OceanSim, an underwater simulation to improve seafloor mapping capabilities and generate synthetic data to reduce the sim-to-real gap. This work was recognized with a CRA Outstanding Undergraduate Researcher Honorable Mention.

Procedural Underwater Terrain Generation for Sim-to-Real (ROB 490)

Independent research with Prof. Katie Skinner building a hybrid terrain generation pipeline for OceanSim. I combined multi-octave Perlin noise with physics-based sediment models — validated Ripple Index geometry, Fourier harmonics for ripple asymmetry, and procedural discontinuities like dead zones, bifurcations, and pattern breaks — then converted heightmaps into USD meshes for NVIDIA Isaac Sim. A PyTorch/VGG-19 neural style transfer stage layers learned texture from real side-scan sonar imagery onto the synthetic terrain, closing the visual gap for perception training data.

Humanoid Locomotion — RoboCup (Michigan Wolverbots)

I lead reinforcement learning and controls for Michigan's RoboCup humanoid soccer team. The work covers policy training for bipedal walking and balance, sim-to-real transfer onto real hardware, and the control stack that keeps the robot upright during dynamic play.
